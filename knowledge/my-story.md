# My Story

Personal observations, experiences, and symptom patterns with Pityriasis Versicolor.

## Timeline
- Disease appeared suddenly around age ~21
- Have been dealing with it for 7-8 years
- Multiple dermatologists diagnosed PV (via skin scrapes)
- Never fully cleared the affected areas, even with aggressive treatment

## Affected Areas
- **Worst:** Chest, especially under the chest
- **Moderate:** Back (more intense than arms, some flaking), neck, shoulders
- **Mild:** Upper arms, groin area, scalp (barely noticeable)

## Symptoms
- Circular rashes, mostly brown/red
- Skin flakes/peels, very dry, sometimes has cracks
- Itching present — improves as symptoms improve
- Sweat intensifies redness
- After bad outbreaks (carbs + alcohol), scratches skin until it bleeds
- This is a significant quality-of-life issue

## Known Triggers
- Excessive carbohydrate consumption (especially wheat)
- Alcohol consumption
- Both make patches significantly more intense in color (browner/redder)
- Sugar creates a cycle — cravings intensify once you start eating it
- Sweating intensifies redness
- Not showering promptly

## Food-Related Triggers
<!-- added via add-knowledge, 2026-03-31 -->
- Eating trigger foods causes itching within ~10–15 minutes
- Itching persists for several hours once triggered

## What Actually Helps
- **Ketogenic diet** — the ONLY thing that significantly improves the condition
- Even with keto, never fully cleared in 7-8 years
- Regular showering is critical
- Frequent washing of clothes, bedsheets, and towels
- Reducing coffee intake seemed to help

## Best Attempt So Far
- Combined: frequent showers, ketogenic diet, less coffee, frequently changing all fabrics touching skin
- Got quite far and managed to contain the fungus well
- BUT: one meal with carbs or a missed shower and the skin immediately worsened again

## Candida Suspicion
- Intense sugar cravings, especially once in the cycle
- Prior antibiotic use (disrupts gut flora)
- Keto massively improves symptoms — Candida feeds on sugar/carbs
- Cannot fully eradicate the fungus after 7-8 years
- Currently suspecting Candida albicans infection in addition to or instead of pure PV

## Current Regimen
### Topical
- Ketoconazole shower gel (Ketazolin 2%) — 1x mornings daily
- Nizoral 2% cream — applied to affected areas
- pH-neutral shower gel (Balea)

### Oral
- Antifungal tablets (antimycotics)

### Supplements
- Zinc, Probiotics (2-4/day), Inulin, Spirulina, Omega 3, Chia seeds, Garlic capsules

## Diagnostic Doubts
- Standard PV treatment hasn't worked in 7-8 years — is the diagnosis fully correct?
- Could it be PMH instead of or in addition to PV?
- Want to learn to better self-assess differential diagnosis

## Dermatology Report
<!-- added via add-knowledge, 2026-04-01 -->
- **Mycologically confirmed PV** — hyphae and spores in clusters ("Spaghetti and meatballs" / "Hyphen und Sporen in Haufen")
- Clinically consistent presentation, extensive spread
- Diagnosed with **pronounced Xerosis cutis** — skin barrier collapse / severe dryness
- PV classified as a condition with **high relapse tendency** (hohe Rezidivneigung)
- Source: personal dermatology report
