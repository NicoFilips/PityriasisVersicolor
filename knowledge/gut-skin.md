# Gut-Skin Axis — Notes

Notes on the connection between gut health and skin conditions. Not rendered on the website.

## Core Understanding
- Malassezia yeast causes Pityriasis Versicolor (also known as Tinea Versicolor / Kleienpilzflechte)
- Candida diet concept: yeast overgrowth in stomach AND skin may be connected
- Treating only the skin may not be enough — need to address gut as well

## Candida Albicans Hypothesis (March 2026)

Currently suspecting a **Candida albicans infection** in addition to or instead of pure PV.

### What points to Candida:
- **Sugar cravings** — intense, especially once in the cycle of eating sugar, almost impossible to resist
- **Prior antibiotic use** — antibiotics disrupt gut flora and can allow Candida overgrowth
- **Ketogenic diet massively improves symptoms** — Candida feeds on sugar/carbs, starving it helps
- **Cannot fully eradicate the fungus** — even after 7-8 years of aggressive treatment
- **Online forums** report strong connection between Candida and skin manifestations like PV
- Many experience reports suggest treating gut Candida resolved their skin problems

### Connection PV ↔ Candida ↔ Immune System
- Both are fungal conditions — Malassezia (skin) and Candida (gut)
- Weakened immune system could allow both to thrive
- Gut flora imbalance (dysbiosis) may weaken local and systemic immunity
- Carb/sugar intake feeds both fungi simultaneously

### Current Gut Strategy
- Probiotics / gut bacteria (2-4 capsules/day)
- Prebiotics: Inulin, Chia seeds
- Spirulina for immune support
- Garlic capsules (natural antifungal)
- Omega 3 (anti-inflammatory)
- Ketogenic diet
