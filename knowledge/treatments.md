# Treatments — Notes

Personal notes on treatments for Pityriasis Versicolor (also known as Tinea Versicolor / Kleienpilzflechte). Not rendered on the website.

## Current Regimen (as of April 2026)

### Topical
- **Ketoconazole shower gel (Ketazolin 2%)** — antifungal wash, used as often as possible
- **Ketoconazole cream (Ketazolin)** — applied to affected areas, used as often as possible
- **Nizoral 2% cream** — ketoconazole-based cream, applied to affected areas
- **pH-neutral shower gel (Balea)** — used as base shower gel to not irritate skin

### Oral
- **Antifungal tablets (antimycotics)** — prescribed oral treatment

### Supplements
- **Zinc** — for skin health
- **Probiotics capsules (gut bacteria)** — 2-4 per day, for gut flora
- **Inulin** — prebiotic fiber to support probiotic colonization
- **Spirulina** — immune system support
- **Omega 3** — anti-inflammatory
- **Chia seeds** — prebiotic, helps probiotics settle
- **Garlic capsules** (from DM) — natural antifungal properties

## What Actually Helps
- **Ketogenic diet** (complete carb elimination) — the ONLY thing that significantly improves the condition
- Even with keto, never fully cleared the affected areas in 7-8 years
- Regular showering is critical — outbreaks worsen without it
- **Frequent washing of clothes, bedsheets, and towels** — personal observation that this helps (anecdotal)
- Reducing coffee intake seemed to help during last intensive attempt

## Hygiene & Environment Strategy (community-sourced, anecdotal)

Multiple community reports emphasize fabric/environment management:

### Clothing
- Wear **clean clothes every day** — no re-wearing between washes
- This should be baseline regardless of TV

### Towels
- **Use towel only once before washing** — may be overkill, but eliminates recontamination risk
- Requires access to washer/dryer to be practical

### Bedding
- We sleep ~8 hours/night — that's a lot of skin contact
- **Change sheets at least once a week**, ideally twice a week during active treatment
- Consider a **breathable mattress** — one person recommends horsehair mattress (horsehair, cotton, wool — all natural, very breathable)
- The logic: reduce moisture buildup where you sleep, since Malassezia thrives in warm/moist environments

### Laundry Detergent
- Consider switching to a **"clean" / natural detergent** instead of mainstream brands (Tide etc.)
- Example: **Branch Basics** or similar plant-based/fragrance-free detergents
- Logic: clothes ~16 hours/day + bedding ~8 hours/night = skin is in constant contact with whatever residue is on your fabrics
- Harsh chemicals or fragrances in detergent could irritate skin or affect skin flora
- Worth a shot — easy change, low risk

### Overall Principle
The idea across multiple reports: **minimize prolonged contact between skin and fabrics that may harbor yeast.** Whether towels/sheets actually "reinfect" is debated, but keeping everything fresh at minimum reduces the fungal load on surfaces touching your skin.

## Best Attempt So Far
Last intensive attempt included: frequent showers, ketogenic diet, less coffee, frequently changing all fabrics touching skin (clothes, bedsheets, towels). Got quite far and managed to contain the fungus well. BUT: one meal with carbs or a missed shower and the skin immediately worsened again.

## Known Triggers
- Excessive carbohydrate consumption (especially wheat)
- Alcohol consumption
- Both make the patches significantly more intense in color (browner/redder)
- Sugar consumption creates a cycle — sugar cravings intensify once you start eating it
- Sweating intensifies the redness
- Not showering promptly

## Detailed Symptom Description
- Skin flakes/peels slightly, is very dry
- Sometimes skin has cracks
- Circular rashes, typically brown/red
- Sweat makes redness worse
- Itching present on skin

## Affected Areas (detailed)
- **Neck** — affected
- **Chest** — worst area, especially under the chest
- **Shoulders** — affected
- **Back** — moderate, more intense than arms, some flaking
- **Upper arms** — mild
- **Groin area** — mild
- **Scalp** — mild, barely noticeable

## Severity of Outbreaks
- After bad outbreaks (carbs + alcohol), scratches skin until it bleeds
- This is a significant quality-of-life issue

## Community Report: "Full-Body Consistency" Approach (Reddit, anecdotal)

Source: Reddit post from someone claiming to have cleared long-standing TV after many failed attempts. **Not a doctor — entirely anecdotal.**

### The Routine (claimed)
1. Daily showers (non-negotiable)
2. Anti-dandruff shampoo with **zinc pyrithione on the scalp** — key insight: scalp may act as reservoir
3. **Sulfur soap + neem soap** on the body
4. Let products sit for a few minutes before rinsing
5. Dry completely after shower
6. Apply antifungal spray to affected areas after drying
7. Continue for **8-10 weeks minimum**, even beyond visible clearance
8. Maintain routine even after symptoms clear

### Key Claims
- **Scalp as reservoir:** The scalp harbors Malassezia and can reinfect the body if not treated simultaneously
- **Towel recontamination:** Don't reuse towels during early treatment phase
- **Consistency > product:** The discipline of daily full-body treatment matters more than which specific product
- **Dryness is acceptable:** Better to have dry skin from treatment than let the condition persist
- **Treatment must continue past visual clearance** — fungus persists invisibly

### Likely Useful
- Treating the scalp too (aligns with medical understanding that Malassezia lives on scalp)
- Consistency and long treatment duration (PV is known to recur if treatment stops too early)
- Full-body approach rather than just treating visible patches
- Drying completely after showering (moisture promotes fungal growth)

### Likely Bro-Science / Contested
- Towels "reinfecting" you — contested in comments, PV is generally not considered contagious (it's endogenous)
- "Complete solution" framing — overpromises vs. evidence, PV is a chronic condition for many
- Specific product combination (sulfur + neem + zinc pyrithione) — no clinical evidence this combo is superior

### My Takeaway
The useful core message: **recurrent TV needs consistent, full-body antifungal maintenance including the scalp, continued well beyond visible clearance.** The specific product claims and reinfection theories are less reliable. Worth trying the scalp treatment aspect — I already know my scalp is mildly affected.

## Community Report: Rotating Multi-Product Approach (YouTube comment, anecdotal)

Source: YouTube commenter with long-term recurring TV that flares every summer. **Not a doctor — anecdotal.**

### Key Insight: Laundry Sanitization
- **Sanitize your laundry!** — Dermatologists apparently never mention this
- Underwear, bras, bedsheets can harbor excess yeast and cause regrowth
- Use **Lysol laundry sanitizer** (detergent aisle) and/or **Borax** in wash cycle
- This person says it helps "a TON"
- **Note:** This aligns with my own observation that frequent washing of fabrics helps

### Their Rotating Wash Schedule
- **3 days/week:** Nizoral + Selsun Blue together
- **2 days/week:** Katalis soap (salicylic acid + zinc oxide + sulfur)
- **2 days/week:** Noble Formula 2% pyrithione zinc soap
- **Important:** Leave lather on skin for at least 2 minutes

### Daily Topical Creams
- **Clotrimazole (Lotrimin)** — antifungal cream
- **Tolnaftate (Tinactin)** — another antifungal cream
- **Noble Formula hydrocortisone + pyrithione zinc cream** — for inflammation and itch (their patches are pink/itchy)

### Product Availability
- Katalis soap → Amazon
- Noble Formula products → Amazon
- Nizoral, Selsun Blue, Lotrimin, Tinactin → drugstore/supermarket

### What's Interesting
- **Rotating products** rather than using one thing daily — different active ingredients on different days
- **Combining two antifungal creams** (clotrimazole + tolnaftate) — different mechanisms of action
- **Laundry sanitization** as a key overlooked factor — matches my experience
- **2-minute contact time** — letting products sit, not just quick wash-off
- Uses **hydrocortisone** for the inflammatory component — I haven't tried this

### Questions / To Research
- Is rotating antifungal products more effective than consistent single-product use?
- Clotrimazole vs ketoconazole — is one better for TV?
- ~~Tolnaftate~~ — NOT effective against Malassezia, only for dermatophytes. Not relevant for PV.
- ~~Borax in laundry~~ — not relevant for PV treatment.
